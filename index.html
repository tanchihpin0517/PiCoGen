PiCoGen (Piano Cover Generation) project aims to build a automactic piano cover generation system which accepts a music audio as input and generates a piano cover of the music. To explore the shared information between music and its cover, we leverage the lead sheet, a type of music notation which specifies the melody and chords of a song, as the intermediate connection between the music and its cover. In PiCoGen (ver. 1), we divide the generating process into two independent stages: extracting the lead sheet with SheetSage and generating the piano cover conditioned on the extracted lead sheet. In PiCoGen2 (ver. 2), we further explore the possibility of generating piano cover with a end-to-end manner to avoid the information losses result from the two-stage generation process. Both two approaches show the capability of catching intermetiate musical features and generating piano covers.
Please check out the papers and repositories in the above links for more details and try the models.

PiCoGen (ver. 1) is a 2-stage system that extracts the lead sheet from the input audio and generates a piano cover from the previous result. Since the generation process has been separated into two independent stages, there is no requirement for paired data (i.e., music and its cover) for training. (Paper, Github)

PiCoGen2 (ver. 2) is an end-to-end system that generates a piano cover directly from the input audio. Like the previous version, we employ SheetSage as the feature extractor, however, without sampling from the output. Instead, we use the last hidden state of SheetSage as the intermediate feature and pass that into the decoder as the condition. (Paper, Github)
